<p><p>Starch-based plastics are used across various processing methods, including blow molding, extrusion, and injection molding. Blow molding forms hollow shapes like bottles, utilizing air to inflate softened material. Extrusion continuously shapes plastic into profiles or films through a die. Injection molding injects molten starch plastic into molds for complex shapes. Other methods may include thermoforming and compression molding, allowing for versatile applications in packaging, disposable items, and biodegradable products, tapping into the growing demand for sustainable materials.</p></p>
