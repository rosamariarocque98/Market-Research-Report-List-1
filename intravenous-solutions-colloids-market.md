<p><p>Intravenous solutions, particularly colloids, are essential in clinical nutrition. Peripheral Parenteral Nutrition (PPN) delivers nutrients through peripheral veins, suitable for short-term nutritional support with less concentrated solutions. In contrast, Total Parenteral Nutrition (TPN) provides comprehensive nutrition via central veins, ideal for patients needing long-term intravenous feeding, allowing for higher concentrations of nutrients. Both methods are crucial in managing patients who cannot receive nutrition orally, ensuring adequate hydration and nutrient delivery effectively and safely.</p></p>
