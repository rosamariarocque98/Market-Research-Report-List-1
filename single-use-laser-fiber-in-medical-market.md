<p><p>Single-use laser fibers in the medical market are categorized by their diameter, which affects their application in various procedures. Fibers with diameters below 300 μm are typically used for precise, delicate surgeries like ophthalmology. Those in the 300-600 μm range balance flexibility and deliver higher laser energy, making them suitable for general surgery and soft tissue applications. Fibers above 600 μm are designed for more robust treatments such as ablating larger tissues or in urology, providing greater power and efficiency.</p></p>
