In contrast, liquid electrolytic capacitors use a liquid electrolyte, generally providing higher capacitance values but at the cost of larger sizes and potential leakage issues. Both types are essential for various applications, including automotive, telecommunications, and consumer electronics, driven by the demand for efficient and compact energy storage solutions.</p></p>
