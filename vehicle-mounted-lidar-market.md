<p><p>Vehicle Mounted LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging) is a remote sensing technology mounted on vehicles to capture high-resolution 3D maps of surroundings. The system typically includes LiDAR sensors, GPS, and inertial measurement units (IMUs) for precise positioning and mapping.</p><p>The Vehicle Mounted LiDAR Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.6% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by increasing demand for autonomous vehicles, advancements in LiDAR technology, and rising adoption in smart city projects. The integration of AI and machine learning with LiDAR systems is enhancing data processing capabilities, further fueling market expansion. Additionally, the need for accurate 3D mapping in industries like construction, mining, and agriculture is contributing to market growth.</p><p>Key trends include the development of compact, cost-effective LiDAR systems, the rise of solid-state LiDAR for improved durability, and the growing use of LiDAR in drone-based applications. Challenges such as high costs and regulatory hurdles remain, but ongoing innovations and investments are expected to address these issues, driving the market forward.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Solid State LiDAR</li><li>Mechanical LiDAR</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Vehicle Mounted LiDAR market includes two main types: Solid State LiDAR and Mechanical LiDAR. Solid State LiDAR uses fixed components without moving parts, offering compact designs, higher durability, and lower costs, making it ideal for mass-market automotive applications. Mechanical LiDAR relies on rotating components to scan environments, providing high-resolution, long-range data but with bulkier designs and higher costs, suited for specialized or industrial uses. Both types enhance autonomous driving and mapping with advanced 3D imaging and object detection capabilities.</p></p>
