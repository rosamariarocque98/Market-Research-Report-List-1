<p><ul><li>CMOS</li><li>CCD</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Automotive radar sensors utilize advanced imaging technologies, predominantly CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) and CCD (Charge-Coupled Device), to enhance vehicle safety and autonomy. CMOS sensors are known for lower power consumption, faster processing speeds, and compact designs, making them ideal for real-time applications in advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS). Conversely, CCD sensors deliver high-quality imaging with superior light sensitivity but are typically bulkier and consume more power. Both types are integral in enabling functionalities like collision avoidance, adaptive cruise control, and lane-keeping assistance.</p></p>
