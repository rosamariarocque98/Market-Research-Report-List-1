<p><p>The antibiotics market is segmented into several types based on their chemical structure and mechanism of action. Cephalosporins are broad-spectrum antibiotics effective against various infections. Penicillins target specific bacterial strains. Fluoroquinolones are known for their potency against gram-negative bacteria. Macrolides are effective in treating respiratory infections. Carbapenems are used for multidrug-resistant infections, while aminoglycosides are primarily employed for serious infections due to their efficacy. Sulfonamides are synthetic antibiotics, and the "others" category includes newly developed or less common antibiotics.</p></p>
