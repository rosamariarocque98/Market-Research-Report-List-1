<p><p>The infrared (IR) spectrometers market is segmented into three main types: Mid-Infrared (MIR), Far-Infrared (FIR), and Near-Infrared (NIR). MIR spectrometers, operating between 400 to 7000 cm⁻¹, are widely used for characterizing organic compounds. NIR spectrometers, covering 700 to 2500 nm, are ideal for agricultural and food analysis due to their non-destructive nature. FIR spectrometers, working below 400 cm⁻¹, focus on studying molecular vibrations and rotational transitions, often applied in research and advanced material characterization.</p></p>
