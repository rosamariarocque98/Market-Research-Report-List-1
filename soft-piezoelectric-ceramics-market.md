<p><p>Soft piezoelectric ceramics are materials that exhibit piezoelectric properties while demonstrating improved flexibility and ductility compared to their hard counterparts. These ceramics typically have a perovskite structure and are primarily composed of lead zirconate titanate (PZT) and other doped materials. Additionally, the trend toward miniaturization in electronic components highlights the need for softer materials that can accommodate tighter spaces without compromising performance.</p><p>Moreover, ongoing research and development efforts focused on enhancing the properties of soft piezoelectric ceramics are paving the way for innovative solutions in energy harvesting and sensor applications.
