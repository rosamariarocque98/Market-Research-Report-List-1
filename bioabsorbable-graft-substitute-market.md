<p><p>The bioabsorbable graft substitute market primarily consists of two types: synthetic and natural materials. Synthetic materials include biopolymers like polylactic acid and polyglycolic acid, engineered for strength and predictable degradation. They are ideal for various applications due to their customizable properties. On the other hand, natural materials, derived from collagen or chitosan, offer biocompatibility and promote natural healing processes. Both types are designed to provide temporary support to tissues while gradually dissolving, facilitating tissue regeneration. </p></p>
