<p><p>Automatic Number Plate Recognition (ANPR) cameras utilize two primary types of sensor technologies: CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) and CCD (Charge-Coupled Device). CMOS sensors are known for their low power consumption, faster processing speeds, and cost-effectiveness, making them popular in modern ANPR systems. In contrast, CCD sensors offer superior image quality and low-light performance, which can be crucial for accurate plate recognition in varying lighting conditions. Both technologies cater to different market segments based on performance requirements and budget considerations.</p></p>
