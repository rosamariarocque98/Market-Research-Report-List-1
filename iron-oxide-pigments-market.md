<p><ul><li>Natural</li><li>Synthetic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Iron oxide pigments are classified into two main types: natural and synthetic. Natural iron oxide pigments are derived from natural ores and minerals, offering earthy tones and environmental benefits. They are non-toxic and have good lightfastness, making them popular in applications like paints and coatings. On the other hand, synthetic iron oxide pigments are manufactured through chemical processes, allowing for a wider range of colors, improved consistency, and enhanced performance properties. Both types are widely used in construction, plastics, and cosmetics.</p></p>
